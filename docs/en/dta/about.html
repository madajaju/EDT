About Digital Transformation Architect

Why canon matters

Why this series exists

Digital Transformation Architect is the authoritative architecture series within the Embracing Digital ecosystem. Hosted by Dr. Darren Pulsipher, the podcast establishes a shared architectural canon for digital transformation using the Open Digital Transformation Architecture (ODXA).

Rather than focusing on tools, hype, or weekly trends, the show examines how organizations design, govern, and sustain transformation across strategic, organizational, process, digital, and physical domains. Each episode explores architectural decisions, constraints, and tradeoffs—helping leaders reason clearly about complex change and avoid short-term optimization that undermines long-term success.

This podcast is designed for executives, architects, and public-sector leaders responsible for outcomes that must endure beyond a single technology cycle.

Digital transformation is full of competing definitions, vendor narratives, and inconsistent practices.

DTA exists to establish a stable, architectural canon: shared language, repeatable decisions, and defensible tradeoffs.

How it’s different

  • DTA is canonical: how transformation should be architected (ODXA-based).
  • Architecture in Context is interpretive: synthesizing the news through architectural lenses.
  • EDT Interviews are evidentiary: real leaders and stories that validate patterns and expose lessons.

Host

Dr. Darren Pulsipher connects real-world change to policy, platforms, and architectural decision-making grounded in best practices.
