GEAR: The Transformation Operating System

A systems architecture methodology for digital architects. Governing Enterprise Architecture Realization (GEAR) integrates the O-DXA structural model and the FORGE practice to align your organization's digital core.

The Transformation Operating System (TOS)

By treating transformation as a systems architecture problem, GEAR helps you eliminate the "hidden tax" of fragmentation and bridge the gap between executive strategy and operational reality.

How Domains and Pillars Converge

Convergence: Model Meets Practice

  • The O-DXA domains provide the structural model (The "Where").
  • The FORGE methodology provides the active practice (The "How").
  • The Six Pillars provide evolving technical perspectives (The "What").
  • The Four Dimensions define the alignment surface: People, Process, Policy, and Technology.
  • Transformation is realized when perspectives are integrated across domains using the FORGE practice to maintain alignment on the surface.

The Result: Solutions

A "Solution" isn't a single pillar. It is the integration of all six capabilities to deliver a durable mission outcome.

This prevents "AI-only" or "Security-only" silos, forcing a whole-system design approach.

The FORGE Process & O-DXA

The FORGE methodology (Find, Observe, Reconcile, Ground, Enhance) is the active engine of GEAR. It is used to navigate and update the O-DXA domains during a transformation.

Mapping the Domains

During the Find and Observe stages, architects map the current state of all five O-DXA domains to identify silos and fragmentation.

Closing the Gaps

The Reconcile stage focuses on aligning the domains—ensuring that strategic intent is reflected in organizational structure and digital platforms.

Real-World Implementation

Ground and Enhance ensure that transformation is rooted in physical and digital reality while augmenting capabilities for the future.
