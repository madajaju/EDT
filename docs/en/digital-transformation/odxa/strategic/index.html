Domain definition

The Strategic Domain serves as the organization's "North Star," aligning direction, priorities, and risk posture with its core mission. It translates long-term aspirations and legislative mandates into actionable strategies, ensuring that every capability and investment is purpose-driven and outcome-focused across the **Transformation Dimensions**.

Key decisions

  • What specific outcomes and public value define our success?
  • Which tradeoffs are acceptable between speed, risk, cost, and resilience?
  • How do we prioritize and fund transformation initiatives across horizons?
  • What governance decision rights and escalation paths ensure accountability?

Common anti-patterns

  • Technology-led strategy (selecting tools before defining desired outcomes)
  • Unmeasured success (failing to link tactical activities to strategic KPIs)
  • Strategy in a vacuum (creating goals without considering operational or physical constraints)
  • Priority Overload (diluting impact by attempting too many simultaneous strategic shifts)

GEAR Integration & Architect's Map

The Strategic Domain is the primary driver of the GEAR Transformation Operating System, setting the requirements for all other domains and pillars.

FORGE Methodology in Strategy

Architects use FORGE to move from vague executive intent to binding architectural constraints.

Stage Architect's Focus Key Artifacts
Find Identify hidden mandates, legacy policies, and unstated mission priorities. Mandate Map, Stakeholder List
Observe Analyze how current "official" strategy differs from actual investment behavior. Investment Heatmap
Reconcile Align conflicting priorities between departments to create a unified roadmap. Shared Outcome Framework
Ground Anchor new strategic goals in existing legislative or regulatory authority. Policy Alignment Doc
Enhance Augment the mission by introducing new value streams and digital business models. Strategic Roadmap 2.0

Strategic Dimensions Map

How the Four Dimensions are governed within the Strategic Domain.

Dimension Strategic Play Example Check
People Leadership alignment and cultural mission-fit. Do executives share the same definition of success?
Process Governance decision rights and cycle times. Is our funding cycle too slow for digital change?
Policy Legal mandates and regulatory guardrails. Does this strategy comply with data privacy laws?
Technology Technology-agnostic investment direction. Are we funding capabilities or just buying tools?

Pillar-Domain Intersection

Architect's checklist for aligning execution pillars with the Strategic Domain.

Pillar Strategic Requirement Verification Point
AI Define ethical boundaries and value cases. Verify AI ROI against mission outcomes.
Cyber Establish risk tolerance and resilience goals. Check alignment with national security policy.
Data Set data sovereignty and stewardship rules. Ensure data strategy supports transparency goals.
Edge Determine mission-critical local autonomy. Verify sites for decentralized execution.
Computing Standardize platform portability vs lock-in. Check cloud vs on-prem strategic balance.
Comms Define connectivity reach and uptime mandates. Verify interop goals for contested environments.
