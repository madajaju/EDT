Altair, a leading computational intelligence firm, has acquired Cambridge Semantics, a renowned data fabric provider. The acquisition is expected to expand Altair's data analytics and artificial intelligence offerings significantly. The move aims to enable organizations to build advanced analytics ecosystems with AI infusion by integrating Cambridge Semantics' knowledge graph technology with Altair's tools. Microsoft Research Asia has developed VASA-1, an AI model that can generate synchronized animated videos of individuals speaking or singing from a single photo and an existing audio track. With the ability to analyze images and audio using machine learning, it creates realistic videos with precise facial expressions and lip-syncing. The innovation eliminates the need for video feeds and allows virtual avatars to be rendered locally with real-time applications such as video conferencing. However, researchers have opted to withhold code release due to concerns about misuse.

Embracing Digital This Week

Water facilities are increasingly vulnerable to cybersecurity threats, prompting the EPA and White House to urge states to submit plans to address risks by June 28. The proposed legislation seeks to mandate cybersecurity standards, but budget constraints hamper efforts. Hackers, including state-sponsored actors, pose significant risks, leaving many facilities vulnerable despite investments. Safeguarding critical infrastructure remains an ongoing challenge, with persistent vulnerabilities underscoring the difficulty of addressing these threats. The EPA's withdrawal of cybersecurity guidelines highlights the complexity of the issue.

Cisco has launched HyperShield, an AI-powered security system that safeguards applications, devices, and data across various IT environments. This move aims to enhance Cisco's position in the cybersecurity market following its recent acquisition of Splunk. HyperShield provides autonomous segmentation and self-qualifying upgrades to improve security and streamline operations, addressing critical infrastructure needs. Cisco's goal is to become a significant player in the AI security landscape, and this launch will help them achieve that aim amidst growing competition.

The National Security Agency (NSA) has released a Cybersecurity Information Sheet (CSI) with best practices for deploying secure and resilient AI systems. Developed in collaboration with various cybersecurity agencies, including CISA and the FBI, the guidance is aimed at National Security System owners and Defense Industrial Base companies. The CSI provides insights on safeguarding AI systems against malicious activities and is the first release from the NSA's Artificial Intelligence Security Center (AISC) under the Cybersecurity Collaboration Center (CCC). The AISC plans to release more guidance on various AI security topics.
